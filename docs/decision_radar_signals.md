# Decision Radar Signals — Cross-Brand Qualitative Notes

Captured during Phase 2 extraction. These signals are too important to lose but
do not cleanly belong in the schema yet. Intended for a prospective franchise buyer.

## Mosquito Authority (Main Line Brands LLC) — 2025 FDD

**Decision-useful signals:**
- Largest system in the cohort by far: 546 franchised territories (end 2024)
- Lowest initial investment in cohort: $54,000-$127,700
- Offers both Full-Size ($45K fee) and Hometown ($25K fee) franchise tiers
- Franchisor will defer up to 50% of initial fee (36-month note at 8%)
- Average franchisee gross revenue: $464,600 (128 qualifying franchisees, 2024)
- National Marketing Fee not yet implemented but reserved at up to 3%
- System is growing: +5, +10, +7 net units across 2022-2024

**Document quality flags:**
- Item 19 contains a `#REF!` spreadsheet error in the 10+ Full Seasons average cell
- Item 19 Part 2 table totals (129/508) differ slightly from stated population (128/506)

**Disclosure quality notes:**
- Item 19 is unusually rich: full company-owned P&L plus detailed franchised gross
  revenue breakdowns by bracket, territory count, ownership length, and years active
- Company-owned P&L shows negative net income both years (-$6.8K in 2023, -$30.4K in
  2024) after $60K annual amortization — materially affects interpretation of company
  location as economic benchmark

**Regulatory history:**
- 5 concluded predecessor (TMA) regulatory actions for selling franchises without state
  registration in MD, RI, VA, WI, and WA (2013-2020). All pre-current-entity. Pattern
  suggests predecessor had lax compliance posture that was cleaned up under new ownership.

**Unusual fee structure:**
- Technology fee is tiered by trailing 12-month gross revenue ($100-$1,200/month)
- Very low transfer fee ($7,500 or $2,500 if existing relationship)

## Mosquito Hunters (Mosquito Hunters, LLC) — 2025 FDD

**Decision-useful signals:**
- System reversed contraction: -4 (2022), -1 (2023), +13 (2024). Zero terminations
  in 2024 after 17 in 2022 and 11 in 2023. Significant turnaround signal.
- Now a "combination" franchise: Pest Hunters + Mosquito Hunters + Humbug Holiday
  Lighting. Two revenue streams instead of one.
- Holiday lighting pilot data (26 SPs): avg $64,727 per franchisee, avg $1,544/customer
- Strong PE backing: Levine Leichtman Capital Partners directors on board

**Document quality flags:**
- Table A.4 customer count totals differ between quantity and value columns in some
  regions — likely reflects some customers with treatments but no calculable value
- No spreadsheet errors detected

**Disclosure quality notes:**
- Item 19 has 7 tables but reports per-treatment pricing and per-customer value by
  region — does NOT disclose total gross revenue per franchise unit. This is the
  weakest Item 19 for assessing unit economics in the cohort.
- 26% of Strategic-Partners excluded from Item 19, including 8 for "failing to invest
  in MH marketing programs." This exclusion may bias performance data upward.

**Unusual fee structure:**
- Marketing floor is very high: greater of $37,500 or 10% of Net Revenues (mandatory)
- Flagged as "Special Risk" on page 4 of the FDD
- 15% additional royalty on out-of-territory revenue (total 25% on OOT revenue)
- Convention fee increased to $2,000/year (from $1,000 in 2024 FDD)

**Notable:**
- All franchise closures across all 3 years coded exclusively as "Terminations" — zero
  Non-Renewals, zero Ceased Operations. Classification methodology is the franchisor's.

## Mosquito Joe (Mosquito Joe SPV LLC) — 2025 FDD

**Decision-useful signals:**
- Second-largest system in cohort: 415 franchised units (end 2024)
- Strongest brand recognition (Neighborly/KKR portfolio)
- 77% customer retention rate, 89% recurring customer rate (Item 19)
- Tiered royalty: 10% up to $500K, drops to 7% above — rewards growth
- Average gross sales per treatment: $90.54

**Document quality flags:**
- Item 19 column header COPY ERRORS: "Annual Number of Customers" and "Annual Number
  of Jobs" tables reuse "Average Annual Gross Sales" column headers. Data values appear
  correct but headers are wrong in the filed FDD. This is disclosure sloppiness.
- First 5 Years median ($287,832) exceeds average ($214,794) — left-skewed distribution.
  Mathematically valid but unusual; a few very low performers drag the average below median.

**Disclosure quality notes:**
- Item 19 provides gross sales by tenure (First 5 Years vs 6+ Years), customer counts,
  job counts, and per-treatment revenue. Useful but does not include a P&L or cost data.
- 24 closed businesses excluded from Item 19 population

**Regulatory/litigation:**
- Franchisor prevailed in franchise dispute (Bailey, Texas 2022-2025) over Direct
  Marketing Program fee non-payment. Franchisee counter-sued alleging fraudulent
  inducement — dismissed. Signal: franchisor will litigate over mandatory marketing fees.

**CRITICAL SIGNAL — 2024 termination spike:**
- 24 terminations in 2024 (vs 5 in 2023, 6 in 2022). Nearly 5x increase.
- Alabama: 8 terminations in one year (went from 9 outlets to 1)
- System contracted by 1 unit for the first time in the reported period
- This is the most significant red flag in the cohort for 2024

**Unusual fee structure:**
- Most complex and highest-cost marketing structure in cohort
- Direct Marketing Program: $37,000/year mandatory (separate from royalty)
- Total first-year marketing spend ~$75,000+
- $2,000 penalty for convention non-attendance
- 6-layer SPV/securitization entity structure (KKR)

## Mosquito Shield (Mosquito Shield Franchise, LLC) — 2025 FDD

**Decision-useful signals:**
- Fastest-growing system: +74 (2022), +38 (2023), +28 (2024)
- Now 435 franchised outlets in 441 territories
- Lowest royalty rate in cohort: 8% (flat, not tiered)
- Company-owned adjusted EBITDA: 29.5% of revenue (percentage only, no absolute revenue)
- 85% customer retention, $715 avg revenue per customer
- System-wide sales growing: $19.4M (2021) to $25.7M (2024)

**Document quality flags:**
- Table 1 vs Table 3 ONE-UNIT DISCREPANCY: 2022 franchised start is 295 (Table 1) vs
  296 (Table 3). Table 1 Total row also appears to exclude company-owned from start count.
- PDF text rendering issue: "PNenewns yJelvrsaenyia" (garbled "Pennsylvania") in Table 2

**Disclosure quality notes:**
- Item 19 exclusion rate is highest in cohort: 26 "non-conforming" franchisees excluded
  (21% of 125 total). Only 81 outlets in gross sales data. This significantly biases
  reported performance upward. What does "non-conforming" mean? Not defined.
- Company-owned P&L shows only cost percentages, not absolute revenue — less useful
  than Mosquito Squad's full dollar P&L or Mosquito Authority's full P&L

**CRITICAL SIGNAL — High termination rates:**
- 65 terminations in 2023, 44 in 2024 — highest absolute numbers in cohort
- Represents ~10-16% of start-of-year franchised count
- Combined with rapid growth (73-103 openings/year), this suggests significant churn:
  system is growing but also losing outlets at a high rate

**Unusual fee structure:**
- Highest local ad floor in cohort: greater of $50,000 or 10% of gross revenues (annually)
- Minimum Gross Sales performance requirements with escalating thresholds ($45K Y1 to
  $283.5K Y5+). Failure allows franchisor to collect 7% of shortfall. Punitive.
- Mandatory bookkeeping vendor ($200-$500/month)
- Legal entity changed from Corporation to LLC in 2022 — source inventory needs update

**Notable:**
- Massachusetts went from 14 to 39 outlets in 2023 (+25 in one year) — unusual
  single-state concentration, worth understanding if territory sales or brand strategy

## Mosquito Squad (Mosquito Squad Franchising SPE LLC) — 2025 FDD

**Decision-useful signals:**
- Highest systemwide revenue in cohort: $124.3M (2024), $100.3M franchised
- Average per-territory gross revenue: $484,506 (207 territories, highest in cohort)
- Most comprehensive Item 19: 7 tables including quartile revenue, close rates,
  renewal rates, per-appointment revenue, per-customer revenue, 5-year growth,
  same-store growth, and full company-owned P&L
- Company-owned P&L: $20.8M revenue across 14 territories, 25.9% net margin —
  best unit economics benchmark in cohort
- Same-store growth: 5% (195 territories, 2023 vs 2024)
- 70% average renewal rate, 48% close rate

**Document quality flags:**
- Item 19 Table 7 is labeled "Table 9" in the FDD text — table numbering error
- No spreadsheet errors or reconciliation issues detected otherwise

**Disclosure quality notes:**
- Best Item 19 in the cohort by a significant margin. Only brand to provide close
  rates, renewal rates, per-appointment revenue, and a full dollar-amount company-owned
  P&L. Company-owned scale (14 territories, $20.8M) provides credible benchmark.
- Item 19 population for detailed metrics (Tables 2-5) is 75 franchisees / 183
  territories — subset that reported via CRM and submitted P&L. Reasonable filter.

**Regulatory/litigation:**
- Massachusetts AG Assurance of Discontinuance (Sep 2024) re: marketing claims about
  barrier spray being "natural," "all-natural," providing health benefits, "EPA-approved."
  Not an admission. $13,000 cost. 10-year AOD. Signal: marketing language constraints
  may affect lead generation for MA franchisees; broader industry risk if other AGs follow.

**Unusual fee structure:**
- Brand Fund is flat monthly fee ($150-$450 escalating) — NOT percentage-based. Unique.
- Tiered royalty: 10%/9%/8% by revenue bracket
- Aggressive termination penalty: liquidated damages = greater of 2 years royalty or $50,000
- Item 7 Additional Funds covers 12 months (vs 3 months for most competitors) — makes
  total investment range appear higher ($162K-$220K) but is actually more conservative
  and honest about working capital needs
- Minimum royalty escalates to $3,000/month by year 9+

**Notable:**
- Franchised revenue declined -6% in 2022 (driven by 14 territory reacquisitions in
  Dec 2021 and terminations), but recovered in 2023-2024
- 15 company-owned territories (MA 9, NH 3, RI 2) — largest company-owned footprint
  in cohort, stable across all 3 years

## Cross-Brand Comparison Signals

### Disclosure Quality Ranking (best to worst Item 19 for buyer decision-making)
1. **Mosquito Squad** — 7 tables, full company-owned P&L, close/renewal rates, per-unit revenue
2. **Mosquito Authority** — Full company-owned P&L + detailed franchised gross revenue distributions
3. **Mosquito Joe** — Gross sales by tenure, retention metrics, per-treatment revenue (no P&L)
4. **Mosquito Shield** — Gross sales quartiles + cost percentages only (21% exclusion rate)
5. **Mosquito Hunters** — Per-treatment pricing by region only, no gross revenue per unit, 26% exclusion

### Document Quality Issues Found
- Mosquito Authority: `#REF!` spreadsheet error, minor population count mismatch
- Mosquito Joe: Column header copy errors (3 tables)
- Mosquito Shield: Table 1/3 discrepancy, garbled PDF text
- Mosquito Squad: Table numbering error
- Mosquito Hunters: Customer count discrepancies between qty/value columns

### FDD Rigor Assessment
- **Most rigorous:** Mosquito Squad (most comprehensive disclosure, minor table numbering issue only)
- **Solid:** Mosquito Authority (rich disclosure despite predecessor regulatory baggage)
- **Mixed:** Mosquito Joe (good data but copy errors and 24-termination spike)
- **Concerns:** Mosquito Shield (high exclusion rate, high churn despite growth, table discrepancies)
- **Weakest disclosure:** Mosquito Hunters (no gross revenue per unit, high exclusion rate, marketing floor risk)
