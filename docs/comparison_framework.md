# Comparison Framework — Mosquito Pest Control Franchise Cohort

**Cohort:** Mosquito Authority, Mosquito Hunters, Mosquito Joe, Mosquito Shield, Mosquito Squad
**Source:** 2025 FDDs filed with Wisconsin DFI
**Date:** 2026-03-14

---

## Purpose

This document defines which dimensions are safe for direct cross-brand comparison,
which require caveats, and which should not be compared yet. It governs what the
product can defensibly present to prospective franchise buyers.

---

## A. Dimensions Safe for Direct Comparison

### 1. Initial Franchise Fee (Single Territory)

| Brand | Fee | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Mosquito Authority | $25,000–$45,000 | Hometown ($25K) vs Full-Size ($45K) |
| Mosquito Hunters | $107,000 | $50K license + $57K training/supply |
| Mosquito Joe | $42,500 | Standard single territory |
| Mosquito Shield | $54,500 | First territory |
| Mosquito Squad | $50,000 | Standard territory (350K–500K pop) |

**Posture: Directly comparable**

Rationale: All brands disclose a clear initial franchise fee for a single territory
in Item 5. Territory sizes vary, but the fee itself is a hard number a buyer must pay.
The comparison is valid as long as territory size differences are noted alongside.

### 2. Total Initial Investment Range (Item 7)

| Brand | Low | High | Midpoint | Additional Funds Period |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Mosquito Authority | $54,000 | $127,700 | $90,850 | 3 months |
| Mosquito Hunters | $141,295 | $170,743 | $156,019 | 3 months |
| Mosquito Joe | $150,155 | $192,075 | $171,115 | 3 months |
| Mosquito Shield | $120,525 | $157,950 | $139,238 | 3 months |
| Mosquito Squad | $162,380 | $220,375 | $191,378 | **12 months** |

**Posture: Comparable with caveat**

Rationale: All brands disclose Item 7 total investment ranges. However, Mosquito Squad
includes 12 months of Additional Funds vs 3 months for everyone else. This makes
Squad's range appear $60K–$80K higher than it would on an apples-to-apples basis.
The comparison must note this structural difference prominently.

**Caveat text:** "Mosquito Squad's Item 7 includes 12 months of working capital
reserves vs 3 months for all other brands. Adjusting Squad to a 3-month basis would
reduce its range by approximately $55K–$85K, making it more comparable to mid-cohort."

### 5. Termination Counts (Item 20 Table 3)

| Brand | 2022 | 2023 | 2024 |
|---|---|---|---|
| Mosquito Shield | 13 | 65 | 44 |
| Mosquito Joe | 6 | 5 | 24 |
| Mosquito Hunters | 17 | 11 | 0 |
| Mosquito Squad | 12 | 6 | 0 |
| Mosquito Authority | 0 | 0 | 0 |

**Posture: Comparable with caveat**

Rationale: Termination counts are directly from Item 20 Table 3. However, brands

**Caveat text:** "Closure classification varies by brand. To compare total attrition

## B. Dimensions NOT Safe for Direct Comparison Yet

### 1. Average Gross Revenue Per Franchise Unit

**Posture: Not comparable yet**

| Brand | Reported Metric | Population | Exclusion Rate |
|---|---|---|---|
| Mosquito Authority | $464,600 avg (per franchisee, multi-territory) | 128 of 154 franchisees | 17% |
| Mosquito Hunters | Per-treatment and per-customer value only | 63 of 85 SPs | 26% |
| Mosquito Joe | $214,794 avg / $287,832 median (first 5 yrs) | 387 of 415 businesses | 7% |
| Mosquito Joe | $432,954 avg / $339,460 median (6+ yrs) | (same pool, by tenure) | |
| Mosquito Shield | $285,839 avg / $134,918 median | 81 of 125 outlets | 21% |
| Mosquito Squad | $484,506 avg per territory | 207 of 226 territories | 8% |

**Why not comparable:**
- **Unit of measurement differs:** Authority reports per franchisee (multi-territory),
  Squad reports per territory, Joe splits by tenure, Shield reports per outlet,
  Hunters does not report total revenue at all.
- **Exclusion rates vary wildly:** 7% to 26%. Higher exclusion biases upward.
- **Population definitions differ:** "Franchisees" vs "territories" vs "outlets" vs
  "Strategic-Partners."
- **Tenure mix is uncontrolled:** A system with more mature units will naturally
  show higher averages.

### 2. Marketing/Advertising Total Burden

**Posture: Not comparable yet**

| Brand | Structure Summary | Estimated First-Year Marketing Cost |
|---|---|---|
| Mosquito Authority | Local $5,500 min or 5%. National reserved (0% now). | ~$5,500–$25,000 |
| Mosquito Hunters | Greater of $37,500 or 10% of Net Revenues | ~$37,500 |
| Mosquito Joe | MAP 2% + DMP $37K + Local $35K + SEO $3.9K | ~$75,000+ |
| Mosquito Shield | Brand Fund 2% + Local $50K min or 10% | ~$50,000+ |
| Mosquito Squad | Brand Fund $150–$450/mo + Local $35K–$50K + Web $350/mo | ~$40,000–$55,000 |

**Why not comparable as a single number:**
- Structures differ radically: percentage-based vs dollar-floor vs flat-fee vs hybrid.
- Some are percentage-of-revenue (scales with success), some are fixed (hurts
  early-stage franchisees disproportionately).
- Some include mandatory vendor programs (Joe's DMP), others don't.
- Cannot reduce to a single "marketing rate" without making false equivalences.

**What IS possible:** A qualitative ranking of marketing burden severity, and
estimated first-year dollar costs. This should be presented as a range, not a rate.

### 3. Total Ongoing Fee Burden (Royalty + Marketing + Technology)

**Posture: Not comparable yet**

All five brands have `total_ongoing_fee_rate: null` in the derived layer because
the marketing and/or technology components cannot be expressed as single percentages.
A combined rate comparison would require normalizing at a specific revenue level
(e.g., "at $300K gross revenue, total fees would be..."), which is a Phase 3
modeling task.

### 4. Company-Owned P&L Benchmarks

**Posture: Not comparable yet**

| Brand | Company-Owned Data | Scale |
|---|---|---|
| Mosquito Authority | Full dollar P&L | 1 territory, $384K revenue, negative net income |
| Mosquito Squad | Full dollar P&L | 14 territories, $20.8M revenue, 25.9% net margin |
| Mosquito Shield | Cost percentages only | 1 location, no absolute revenue |
| Mosquito Joe | None | — |
| Mosquito Hunters | None | — |

**Why not comparable:**
- Only 2 brands provide full dollar P&Ls, and they are at completely different scales
  (1 territory vs 14 territories).
- Mosquito Shield provides only cost percentages, not dollar amounts.
- Company-owned locations do not pay royalties, making them structurally different
  from franchised units.
- Mosquito Authority's company-owned location shows negative net income (after $60K
  amortization), while Squad's 14-territory operation shows 25.9% margin. These are
  not comparable benchmarks.

### 5. Customer Retention / Recurring Revenue Rates

**Posture: Comparable with caveat (limited subset)**

| Brand | Retention Metric | Methodology |
|---|---|---|
| Mosquito Joe | 77% customer retention, 89% recurring | System-wide, 410 businesses |
| Mosquito Shield | 85% returning YoY | Software-dependent (57% had data) |
| Mosquito Squad | 70% renewal rate | 75 franchisees via CRM |

Mosquito Authority and Mosquito Hunters do not report retention metrics.

**Caveat:** Definitions differ ("retention" vs "renewal" vs "returning"). Populations
differ. These can be presented as brand-specific signals but not as a clean ranked
comparison.

## D. Product-Facing Comparison Strategy

### What the first public comparison pages should compare:
1. **Initial cost to enter** — franchise fee + investment range (with Squad caveat)
2. **System trajectory** — net unit growth, total attrition, system size trend
3. **Disclosure quality** — a proprietary signal that differentiates the product
4. **Royalty structure** — presented as structure descriptions, not single numbers
5. **Litigation/regulatory** — factual, low-risk to present

### What they should avoid comparing yet:
1. Revenue per unit (too many methodology differences)
2. "Total cost of ownership" (requires fee modeling at revenue levels)
3. Profitability (only 2 company-owned P&Ls, incomparable scales)
4. "Best value" rankings (requires normalization not yet done)

### What should be framed as "signals" rather than conclusions:
1. Marketing burden severity (qualitative ranking, not precise numbers)
2. Customer retention indicators (directional, not ranked)
3. Termination spikes (flagged as events, not permanent brand scores)
4. Disclosure quality ranking (evidence-based opinion, not hard metric)

### Safest/strongest first wedge for buyer-facing value:
**"System health trajectory"** — combining net unit growth + attrition rate + system
size over the 3-year window. This uses the most comparable data, requires no
normalization, and directly answers the buyer question: "Is this system growing
healthily or shrinking?"

Second wedge: **"What will this actually cost me to start?"** — initial fee +
investment range, clearly presented with structural notes.

Third wedge: **"How transparent is this franchisor?"** — disclosure quality as a
differentiated editorial signal that no competitor product offers.
