# Decision Report Specification — v1

**Date:** 2026-03-14

---

## What the report is

A single-brand decision-preparation document for a prospective franchise buyer
who has narrowed to one brand and needs to understand what the FDD actually says
before committing. ~2,500–3,500 words. 10–15 minute read.

## Format

Self-contained HTML file, print-friendly, no external dependencies. Can be
opened in a browser, saved, or printed to PDF. Styled for reading, not for
browsing — no navigation, no interactive elements, no links to the public site.

## Tone

Calm, direct, analytical. Reads like a memo from a sharp advisor who has
read the FDD and the four competing FDDs. Not legal advice. Not a
recommendation. Decision-useful information presented honestly.

## How it differs from the free pages

| Free site | Paid report |
|---|---|
| Shows totals and ranks | Breaks down components and explains why they matter |
| Flags watchouts as bullet points | Explains what each watchout means for the decision |
| Lists fee burden at 4 levels | Models the escalation path and identifies triggers |
| Says whether Item 19 is disclosed | Translates what the Item 19 data actually implies |
| Shows system health trend | Interprets what the closure pattern suggests |
| — | Provides discovery day questions tied to specific findings |

The report is synthesis and interpretation, not additional data display.

## Sections

1. **Header** — Brand name, filing year, source, date, disclaimer
2. **Executive Summary** — 4–6 sentences. What stands out, biggest risk, biggest
   strength, peer position.
3. **Fee Burden Analysis** — Component breakdown at 4 revenue levels. What drives
   cost. Escalation triggers. Comparison to cohort range.
4. **Item 19 Interpretation** — What is disclosed, what it implies, what it omits,
   what a new operator should and should not infer.
5. **Investment Breakdown** — Where the money goes. What is structurally fixed vs
   variable. Material footnotes.
6. **System Health** — Growth quality narrative. Closure patterns. What the
   trajectory suggests.
7. **Risk Signals** — Regulatory history, entity structure, document quality,
   disclosure concerns. Not exhaustive legal review — focused on decision-relevant
   signals.
8. **Peer Context** — Where this brand sits in the cohort. Strongest contrasts.
   Not a ranking — a positioning.
9. **Questions for Discovery Day** — 8–12 specific, data-derived questions to ask
   the franchisor.
10. **Source & Methodology** — Data provenance, extraction method, modeling
    assumptions, disclaimer.

## What to standardize vs. keep brand-specific

**Standardized:** Section structure, header, methodology, disclaimer, fee model
presentation format, peer context framing.

**Brand-specific:** Executive summary, Item 19 interpretation, risk signals,
discovery day questions, watchout narratives. These must be written per brand —
they cannot be templated from data alone.

## Provenance posture

The report does not use notation tags (Extracted/Modeled/Editorial) inline like
the site. Instead, the methodology section explains the data layers once.
Specific claims reference FDD page numbers where useful (e.g., "Item 6, p. 15").

## Disclaimers

- Not legal advice, not investment advice
- Based on publicly filed 2025 FDD (Wisconsin DFI)
- Modeled values use stated assumptions
- Buyer should read the actual FDD and consult professionals

## What "good enough for v1" means

- Accurate to the extracted data
- Genuinely useful to someone evaluating this specific brand
- Clearly distinct from the free pages (deeper, more interpretive)
- Worth $99 to someone about to invest $100K+
- Does not need to be beautifully designed — needs to be well-written and clear
